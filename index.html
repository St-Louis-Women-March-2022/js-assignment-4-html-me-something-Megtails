Anime I Forced My Mom to Watch During Quarantine

At the end of 2019, I had been actively searching for a home. Come March 2020, the world had turned upside down and I found myself instead quaranined with my 60-year-old mother. In a desparate need for some sort of comfort during such trying times, I turned to an old friend... anime. While I was a big fan during middle school, my mother had never actually watched any anime before.

And thus began the journey of my mother experiencing anime for the first time.

Here are the results:

Anime

Skip Beat!

General Information

  • Episode total: 25
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Romantic Comedy
  • Air Date: 2008-2009

Summary

A 16-year-old girl is spurned by her childhood friend and pseudo-boyfriend (who happens to be an up-and-coming pop sensation). She embarks on a quest for revenge—vowing to become even more famous than he is. And thus she embarks into the wacky world of acting.

Verdict

Rating: 8/10

Despite only giving Skip Beat! an 8/10, she went on to read EVERY. SINGLE. VOLUME. of the manga that has been published in English. That is a total of 42 volumes. To give some perspective, this manga series has been in publication for 20 years. To date, it is the only anime she liked enough to read the manga version.


Fruits Basket

General Information

  • Episode total: 63
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Romance/Supernatural
  • Air Date: 2019-2021

Summary

A young woman finds herself caught up in a family afflicted by a curse. Select members of said family transform into zodiac animals when embraced by a member of the opposite sex. Despite how silly it sounds, the story is one focused on healing from generational trauma.

Verdict

Rating: 10/10

This one made us both cry multiple times. The manga was a favorite of mine from childhood and I was delighted to see it finally get the full adaptation it deserves. Mom's favorite character is Hatsuharu, the character who turns into a cow (which happens to be her favorite animal).


Ouran High School Host Club

General Information

  • Episode total: 26
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Reverse Harem
  • Air Date: 2006

Summary

A poor high school student finds herself indebted to the school's Host Club and must pretend to be a boy in order to pose as a host herself and work off her debt.

Verdict

Rating: 8/10

"It's kind of naughty." I can't say I don't understand where she's coming from. We actually ended up watching this one twice: once in Japanese with subtitles and once in English. She ended up enjoying it much more in English (the rating, however, reflects her initial impression). She even requests that I put on a random episode here and there when she wants a short something to lighten the mood.


Yuri on Ice

General Information

  • Episode total: 12
  • Demographic: Josei
  • Genre: Sports
  • Air Date: 2016

Summary

An underdog ice skater from Japan has the opportunity of a lifetime when his childhood idol offers to become his new skating coach.

Verdict

Rating: 10/10

This quickly became my mother's comfort anime, which I frankly did not see coming. She has requested we watch it about 5 times over the last two years (and I've indulged her at least 3 of those times). She is enamored with the art style and the beautiful animation. The heartfelt story is the cherry on top.


Cardcaptor Sakura

General Information

  • Episode total: 70
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Magical Girl
  • Air Date: 1998-2000

Summary

An elementary school student discovers a book of magical cards in her basement. The cards are unleashed upon the world and she accepts the mantle of Cardcaptor in order to capture them once more.

Verdict

Rating: 10/10

This charming anime is hard to dislike, even as an adult. However, I think her rating may have been skewed by the knowledge that this is my all-time favorite. It was very important to me during my formative years and remains one of the most influential pieces of media in my life.


Maid Sama!

General Information

  • Episode total: 26
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Romantic Comedy
  • Air Date: 2010

Summary

The straight-laced class president has a secret—she works at a maid cafe on the side to earn money to support her family. She does this on the other side of town so no one will find out her secret, but then the most popular guy in school just happens to find out.

Verdict

Rating: 9/10

It is refreshing to have a male lead who is so secure in his affection for the leading lady in such a short amount of time. Normally that gets dragged out forever. This is another one that we've come back to on a casual basis for when we need something short, sweet, and funny to watch as a palate cleanser.


Azumanga Daioh

General Information

  • Episode total: 26
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Slice of Life/Comedy
  • Air Date: 2002

Summary

A chronicle of the everday life of six high school girls and two of their teachers.

Verdict

Rating: 8/10

The surreal humor took some getting used to for my mom, but she was swept up in their friendships and the bite-sized plots. She was also very enamored with one of the characters' pet dog, who can be seen flying through the air during the show's outro.


Death Note

General Information

  • Episode total: 37
  • Demographic: Shōnen
  • Genre: Thriller
  • Air Date: 2006-2007

Summary

Light Yagami, a genius high school student, finds a Death Note that has been dropped into the human world by a god of death. If someone's name is written into the Death Note, that person will die shortly thereafter (seemingly of a heart attack unless otherwise specified). Light uses the notebook many times and soon a cat-and-mouse pursuit begins between himself and the famous detective known only as "L."

Verdict

Rating: 10/10

I had been unsure how my mother would feel about an anime that was such a departure from our usual fare. This is a far cry from our romantic comedies and other lighthearted stories. But she was completely enthralled the entire time and eager for more. Despite sometimes mistakenly calling it "Murder Book" instead of Death Note, this is one of her favorites.


Monthly Girls' Nozaki-kun

General Information

  • Episode total: 12
  • Demographic: Shōjo
  • Genre: Romantic Comedy
  • Air Date: 2014

Summary

A high school girl attempts to confess to her crush that she likes him, but he mistakenly believes her to be a fan and instead hands her his autograph. It turns out he is a popular shojo manga artist who works under a pen name on the side. She then agrees to become his assistant.

Verdict

Rating: 9/10

This was a rare instance where I had actually not seen the anime already before we started watching it together. It was a treat for the both of us. Though the romantic subplot is a tad unfulfilling, the comedy is top-tier.


Demon Slayer

General Information

  • Episode total: 44
  • Demographic: Shōnen
  • Genre: Supernatural
  • Air Date: 2019-Present

Summary

When a young man's family is slaughtered by demons, he joins a secret organization called the Demon Corps. where he is trained to fight demons. He does all this in the hopes of finding a way to turn his beloved sister, who had been transformed into a demon during the attack, back into a human.

Verdict

Rating: 10/10

This is the only anime that we are in the process of watching that is still being aired. Despite the dark premise, there is a surprising amount of heart to this story. I find that shonen written by women is a particular sweet-spot for me. It would seem this also rings true for my mother.

Anime Demographics

A lot of people hear "shojo" or "shonen" and think they are a genre of anime or manga. In actuality, they are the demographic. Let's break that down:

  • Shōjo: Girls and Young Adult Women
  • Shōnen: Boys and Male Teens
  • Josei: Adult Women
  • Seinen: Young Adult Men and Adult Men
